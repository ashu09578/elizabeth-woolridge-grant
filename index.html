Eras

LANA DEL REY

sadness • glamour • americana

Born to Die (2012)

Lana Del Rey’s breakthrough era, defined by cinematic production, tragic romance, and nostalgic Americana. The album introduced her signature melancholic persona and visuals.

Ultraviolence (2014)

A darker, guitar-heavy era exploring toxic love, self-destruction, and raw emotion. The album showcased a more unfiltered and intense side of Del Rey’s artistry.

Norman F***ing Rockwell! (2019)

A critically acclaimed album praised for its songwriting, introspection, and cultural commentary, often considered her artistic peak.

Brooklyn Baby (2014)

“Brooklyn Baby” captures Lana Del Rey’s ironic take on indie culture, cool-girl detachment, and modern romance. Blending dreamy vocals with hazy production, the song contrasts intellectual posturing with emotional vulnerability, becoming an anthem of effortless cool and quiet longing.

Chemtrails Over the Country Club(2021)

An era of quiet rebellion, soft glamour, and suburban melancholy. Lana trades excess for restraint — exploring freedom, femininity, and intimacy beneath manicured lawns and whispered conversations.
