Eras

LANA DEL REY

sadness • glamour • americana

Born to Die (2012)

Lana Del Rey’s breakthrough era, defined by cinematic production, tragic romance, and nostalgic Americana. The album introduced her signature melancholic persona and visuals.

Ultraviolence (2014)

A darker, guitar-heavy era exploring toxic love, self-destruction, and raw emotion. The album showcased a more unfiltered and intense side of Del Rey’s artistry.

Norman F***ing Rockwell! (2019)

A critically acclaimed album praised for its songwriting, introspection, and cultural commentary, often considered her artistic peak.

Brooklyn Baby (2014)

“Brooklyn Baby” captures Lana Del Rey’s ironic take on indie culture, cool-girl detachment, and modern romance. Blending dreamy vocals with hazy production, the song contrasts intellectual posturing with emotional vulnerability, becoming an anthem of effortless cool and quiet longing.

Art Deco (2015)

A sleek, hollow-glamour anthem capturing Lana’s disillusionment with youth culture, fame, and the party scene of Los Angeles. “Art Deco” reflects the emptiness behind flashing lights — cool, detached, and quietly tragic.

Dark paradise(2012)

Dark Paradise is a haunting, melancholic song about loving someone who is gone—emotionally or physically—and being unable to let that love die. Lana sings from the perspective of someone who feels trapped between reality and memory, where happiness only exists in dreams or an imagined afterlife.

Honey moon(2015)

explores the themes of toxic romance, faded glamour, and the desperate, fleeting nature of bliss. It acts as a cinematic, jazz-influenced reflection on love, obsession, and self-reflection

White dress(2021)

an autobiographical, nostalgic look back at her life at age 19, before achieving fame. Working as a waitress, she felt free and authentic, reminiscing about a simpler time that she now views as more idyllic than her current, heavily scrutinized
